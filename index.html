about me

i have a background in mechanical engineering and data science. for the last five years i've been helping organizations figure out how to deploy AI so it actually helps them — which in practice means running workshops, sorting through which use cases are real and which ones only look good in a deck, and building the systems that make the whole thing stick. the gap between proof-of-concept and something people actually use is where most of the interesting problems live.

that gap is about to get a lot harder to close. AGI is probably 18 months away, and i think a lot about what that means — how organizations get restructured around it, how it diffuses through society, and what humans actually do on the other side of that. no good answers yet.
